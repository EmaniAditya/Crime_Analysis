Multiple linear regression is used to estimate the relationship between two or more independent variables and one dependent variable.

Crime Rate:


Crime Rate is defined as the Crime reported per 100k population of States/UTs which have been arrived at using the Mid-Year Projected Population of respective States/UTs of 2021 based on 2011 census (Report of the Technical Group on Population Projections, July, 2020, National Commission on Population, Ministry of Health & Family Welfare).

Crime Rate is a result of socio-economic factors.

Here we are using Multiple Linear Regression for predicting the crime rate of India (Dependent Variable) as a factor of socio-economic parameters (independent variables ) such as :



  • Unemployment Rate (%): The unemployment rate is the percentage of the total labor force that is unemployed but actively seeking employment and willing to work.

  • Population(in cr): Population projections from World Bank.

  • Literacy rate : The literacy rate refers to the percentage of the population of a certain age group who can read and write.

  • GDP(per capita) : Gross domestic product (GDP) per capita is an economic metric that breaks down a country's economic output per person.
